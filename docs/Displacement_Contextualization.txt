About ten years ago I was spending a lunch break outside, just enjoying the day, thinking about gravity — specifically about what it would mean if gravity were something being displaced rather than something being curved. I'm not saying I had an epiphany. I was just one person with no real expertise in this field, curious about stuff.
Most of my ruminations led to conclusions that already existed. There wasn't anything new the displacement idea seemed to do. Over the years I kept coming back to it anyway, occasionally asking myself what this or that would mean in a universe where spacetime is displaced. When ChatGPT arrived, this was one of the first things I explored — and I hit dead end after dead end. Anything that shook out kept running into mutually exclusive conditions, or just reinvented the wheel. Many different AIs, same conclusion: my thinking was either in error or offered nothing new.
The idea of rotational displacement came up in a Claude conversation several months before the work in this repo. That particular conversation didn't resolve into anything meaningful, but I kept the idea. It fit well alongside the original radial displacement concept I'd been carrying.
The connection that opened everything up was a question I'd been forming about moiré patterns I was seeing in my window screens. I asked Claude whether it was possible to treat the two screens as position and momentum — with one side as ground truth and the other as our view of it — and back-compute the ground truth from what we can see, given knowledge of the screen composition. Claude worked through the moiré calculations and noticed they were the same structure as the Wigner characteristic function. That was the bridge.
I am the human behind this repo. I realize these claims, developed with overt AI assistance, make this a prime candidate for dismissal — and for attribution of the stranger ideas to hallucination. I can't guarantee there are no hallucinations. But the genesis of the displacement idea is not AI. It is a person sitting outside ten years ago, thinking about what it would mean if something were displaced.
